The Origins of Coffee

Coffee's exact origin is a mystery, but most legends trace its roots to Ethiopia, where it is believed that the coffee plant was first discovered. The story goes that a goat herder noticed his goats became highly energetic after eating coffee berries, and soon the energizing drink was born.

The Spread of Coffee Around the World

Coffee spread to the Middle East, where it became a staple drink in Arab culture. By the 16th century, coffee houses were popping up in cities across the region, and the beverage quickly traveled to Europe. It soon became a cultural and social centerpiece worldwide, influencing trade, politics, and social gatherings.
